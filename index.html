8,000 years of civilisation

Three empires.
One city.

657 BC — AD 330
Byzantion
Greek Colony

Founded by Megarian colonists, Byzantion rose to become one of antiquity's most prized port cities — commanding both shores of the Bosphorus and controlling the vital trade route between East and West for centuries. It laid the foundations for every civilisation that would follow.

AD 330 — 1453
Konstantinopolis
Eastern Roman Empire

Emperor Constantine I refounded the city as the new capital of the Roman Empire. For over a millennium, Constantinople stood as Europe's largest and wealthiest city — crowned by Hagia Sophia, the Hippodrome and the Great Palace, monuments whose grandeur the world had never before witnessed.

AD 1453 — Present
İstanbul
Ottoman Empire & Republic of Türkiye

When Sultan Mehmed II conquered the city in 1453, a new chapter began. Istanbul became the beating heart of the Ottoman Empire — Topkapi Palace, the Süleymaniye Mosque and the Grand Bazaar stand as its extraordinary legacy. Today, a city of 15 million, Istanbul remains the world's most singular meeting of history, culture and modernity.
