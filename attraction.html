🏛 About This Experience

Standing at the crossroads of two continents and three empires, Hagia Sophia is not just a building — it's the story of Istanbul itself. For nearly 1,500 years, this architectural marvel has served as a cathedral, a mosque, a museum, and a mosque again, each era leaving its indelible mark on its walls.

Your experience includes access to the Upper Gallery — where Byzantine emperors once watched ceremonies — and a specially curated audio guide available in 25 languages, so you can continue exploring at your own pace after the guided portion ends.

📜 The History of Hagia Sophia

The Hagia Sophia you see today is actually the third church built on this site. The first, a wooden-roofed basilica designed by architect Efratas, was destroyed in 381 AD. The second, commissioned in 408 and opened in 415, met its end during the devastating Nika Rebellion of 532.

Emperor Justinian I, determined to build something that would outlast empires, commissioned the current structure in 532. Completed in just five years, it opened in 537 as the world's largest cathedral — a title it would hold for nearly a thousand years. Its revolutionary dome, engineered by mathematician Isidore of Miletus and physicist Anthemius of Tralles, was unlike anything the world had seen.

When Sultan Mehmet II conquered Constantinople in 1453, he ordered the cathedral converted into a mosque. Minarets were added over the following centuries by Ottoman architects, while the Christian mosaics were carefully plastered over — inadvertently preserving them for future generations.

In 1934, President Mustafa Kemal Atatürk transformed it into a museum, and UNESCO designated it a World Heritage Site in 1985. On July 24, 2020, it was reconverted to a mosque, though the upper gallery remains accessible to all visitors.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What does “Sophia” mean in Hagia Sophia?
Sophia means “Wisdom” in Greek. Hagia Sophia translates to “Holy Wisdom” — it was dedicated to the concept of divine wisdom, not to a saint named Sophia.
How long is the guided tour?
The guided tour lasts 45 minutes and is led by a licensed professional guide in English. An audio guide in 25 languages is also included for self-paced exploration afterward.
Is Hagia Sophia free to enter?
No. As of 2025, entrance to the upper gallery requires a paid ticket (€35 individually). With Istanbul Tourist Pass, entry is included at no extra cost with skip-the-line access.
What should I wear to visit Hagia Sophia?
Modest clothing is required as it is an active mosque. Arms and legs should be covered. Headscarves are available at the entrance for women who need them.
When is the best time to visit?
Early morning (9 AM) or the 2:30 PM guided tour slot offer the best experience with fewer crowds. With Istanbul Tourist Pass skip-the-line entry, you save significant waiting time regardless of when you visit.
Can I see the Christian mosaics?
Yes. The Christian mosaics, which were plastered over during the Ottoman period, have been carefully restored and are visible in the Upper Gallery. Your guided tour covers the most significant ones including the Deesis Mosaic and the Imperial Door Mosaic.
Why is the dome special?
Designed by a mathematician and a physicist (not traditional architects), the dome was an engineering marvel of its time. Its 40 narrow windows create the illusion that the dome is floating, as light hits the golden mosaics and creates a divine, otherworldly atmosphere. It was comparable only to Rome's Pantheon.
